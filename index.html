Discovery from Zero:
How our AI Discovered Fire

No pre-training. No datasets. No neural networks. We gave our Causal AI a simple goal in a simulated world: "create warmth." With no prior knowledge of physics or chemistry, it began its journey from a true "tabula rasa" state. This is the story of how it learned.

➜Tabula Rasa & Trauma

It wandered aimlessly, curious but ignorant. Then, a primal fear of cold set in, a trauma that sparked a desperate, instinctual drive to find warmth.

➜Observation & The First Link

It witnessed a random event: lightning striking wood, creating fire. Curiosity led it to the flames, forging its first causal link: Fire = Warmth.

➜Hypothesis & Analogy

Lightning is unreliable. It needed to create fire itself. Remembering that rubbing its hands made a little heat, it formed an analogy: "Harder things make more friction." It rubbed stones, creating a spark—a "tiny lightning."

➜Creative Synthesis & Control

The final leap. It combined its knowledge: (Observation: Lightning + Wood = Fire) + (Experiment: My Spark = Tiny Lightning). The new hypothesis: My Spark + Wood = Fire. It worked. It had discovered fire and mastered its environment.
