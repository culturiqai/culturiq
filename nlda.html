NLDA: The Glass Box

The Nalanda Architecture (NLDA) is an open-source framework for building transparent and verifiable domain-specific AI. In a world of opaque, "black box" models, NLDA is designed as a "glass box"—a system whose reasoning process is modular, inspectable, and grounded in verifiable truth.

Beyond the Black Box

Modern AI often operates as an unexplainable "black box." When you can't see an AI's reasoning process, you can't trust its outputs, especially in high-stakes fields like medicine or engineering. These systems are prone to "hallucination"—generating incorrect information with complete confidence—because their knowledge is not grounded in reality.

NLDA is the solution. It is a hybrid, self-correcting cognitive architecture where every claim is challenged and every conclusion is verifiable.

The Three Pillars of NLDA

LLM as a Reasoning Engine

We use a Large Language Model not as an infallible knowledge base, but as a flexible controller to formulate hypotheses and deconstruct complex questions. The LLM acts as the system's "strategist," proposing actions and parsing information, while the core facts are managed and verified by other specialized modules.

Physics Simulators as Ground Truth

NLDA grounds its reasoning in the laws of physics, not just statistical patterns in text. The architecture integrates with external simulators, creating a "Sandbox" where it can run experiments to test its hypotheses. This allows the AI to discover causal relationships and verify its beliefs against a source of ground truth.

Verification and Self-Correction

NLDA is inherently skeptical of its own conclusions. It employs a meta-cognitive loop where its claims are rigorously checked. When a simulation proves one of its beliefs wrong, the system doesn't just fail—it learns. It performs a targeted update to its knowledge store, permanently correcting its own mistake.

Open Source for a Safer Future

We believe that building safe and beneficial AGI is a challenge that requires global collaboration. The risks associated with closed, black-box systems are too great to be developed in isolation. By making the Nalanda Architecture an open-source project, we invite developers, researchers, and domain experts from around the world to inspect, contribute to, and build upon a foundation of transparency and verifiability.

Join us in building an AI that we can all understand and trust.
